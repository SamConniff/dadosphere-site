about

The Dadosphere is a living, anonymous network of real fathers and men answering each other's honest questions. Not a chatbot. Not a helpline. You ask; the most relevant real answer comes back — written by a living man, curated, anonymised. No advice is generated. The only thing the machine does is find the closest answer, then it steps aside.

The cultural proposition: the response to the manosphere is the dadosphere. Give strength to get strength. "GI not AI" — gathered intelligence from 509 real men.

the method

509 men from 25 countries answered ten questions, anonymously. Their answers are held verbatim — never edited, polished, or paraphrased. Nothing appears here until a human has read it. Themes were mapped from the answers themselves, not imposed on them.

the promises

verbatim, always — your words stay your words
anonymous, properly — no names, no emails, nothing traceable
read by a human before it's public
your data stays in the UK / EU, full stop
no accounts, no tracking, no ads, ever

who's behind it

Sam Conniff — a dad in south London, author of Be More Pirate. He kept noticing the questions men don't get asked, so he asked them. 509 men answered. This is what they said.
