What the FAQ

What is the Dadosphere?

A social experiment in collective intelligence and lived experience — built to answer the questions men carry alone. Not a therapy service. Not a chatbot. Not advice. When you ask a question, you receive anonymous lived experience from real men, semantically matched to the themes in what you asked. Adjacent honesty, not easy answers.

How does it work?

You ask a question. The Dadosphere uses semantic matching to find responses from real men whose experience connects to what you're asking about. You get a snippet — not an answer, a reflection — and you can ask for another. You also get three practical signposts: something you can listen to now, something advisory if you want a human, and something specific to your situation. None of it tells you what to do. All of it gives you something to think with.

Who answered?

500+ men who responded to our research — answering ten honest questions across themes from masculinity and fatherhood to struggle, what helped, and legacy. Their answers are anonymised, stored on UK servers, and matched by theme — not by name, age, or any identifying information. Some are dads. Some aren't. All of them were honest.

Is it confidential?

No cookies. No tracking. No sign-up. No data stored about you or your question. What you ask here stays here. Cookies are for monsters.

Who built it?

Sam Conniff — a dad in south London, and the author of Be More Pirate. He kept noticing the questions men don't get asked, so he asked them. 500+ men answered. This is what they said.

Can I contribute?

Yes. Give strength → Share your own lived experience, anonymously, for the next man who asks.
